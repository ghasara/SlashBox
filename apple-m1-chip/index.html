Apple M1 Chip: Tech Revolution is here

The Apple M1 has created an integrated chip with the CPU, GPU and RAM all packed into one with a tiny footprint using 5 nanometre fabrication. This tight integration in a smaller footprint makes it the most efficient chip available for consumers to date.



A CPU or microprocessor chip is the brain of any computer. Intel is the most popular CPU brand today, though the modern CPU — a combination of hardware and software — was introduced by IBM in 1964 as System/360. Introducing software instructions via Complex Instruction Set Computer (CISC) residing within the processor was the breakthrough then. In these early days, there were just the Japanese who had similar technology. In 1968, Japanese engineer Masatoshi Shima from Busicom began designing a CPU which was then taken over by Intel. The two companies jointly released the first CISC-based 4-bit Intel CPU in 1970, almost six years after IBM.

Apple has created an amazing chip with the Apple M1 that packs the CPU and GPU and RAM into a tiny fabric. The tight integration of these components allows for higher processing speeds with less power consumption. Its integrated nature also makes it smaller compared to other chips which makes it more efficient for consumers.

Apple m1 chip a small chip with big capability. The m1 delivers high-bandwidth, low‑latency performance for battery-efficient apps and unlocked performance for when you need it. Every m1 processor is made with the same 32‑nanometer process technology as our A‑series processors.As a result, all of the technologies in the SoC can access the same data without copying it between multiple pools of memory. This dramatically improves performance and power efficiency. Video apps are snappier. Games are richer and more detailed. Image processing is lightning fast. And your entire system is more responsive.



The m1 chip features a new custom 8‑core CPU, designed to power through tasks while using as little power as possible. From editing family photos to exporting iMovie videos for the web to managing large Lightroom libraries, M1 blazes right through it all — with unprecedented battery life.



This triggered a race in the industry to create a new powerful brain CPU. IBM, Intel, Motorola, NEC, Zilog, Toshiba, Fujitsu and a few more got into this race, which was primarily limited to the Americans and Japanese. The Japanese were good with electronics hardware, manufacturing capabilities while the Americans had software and easy access to capital as their advantage.



Apple has just made a huge leap in their newest release of hardware and firmware. With the iDevices 9 operating system comes a new Apple M1 chip which replaces the current Apple A12 chip. The Apple M1 is an entirely new way of computing devices for Apple, meaning you’ll notice some differences right out of the box such as better battery life and more efficient apps that use less data. While these are all welcome upgrades, most people are concerned about how fast is it? How does it compare to the previous iPhone XS using the Apple A12 chip? We’ll get into that later on in this article.

Whether you’re an Apple fanboy or not, there is no way you can deny one thing: Apple knows how to do hardware.Their flagship product, the iPhone, has kept its crown as the most popular phone out there for many years now. In fact, they haven’t introduced any ground-breaking features in the last few generations of iPhones, yet their sales keep going up and up. A large contributing factor to this success is the Apple M1 chip that comes with it.

Is there more to M1?

These systems will also have Artificial Intelligence (AI) built in. AI mimics and creates human brain-like neural networks in these chips. This is what Apple calls Bionic processors, or Neural Processing Units (NPU) in more technical terms. The M1’s NPU makes it fully future ready while optimally packing the entire system into one chip. It is the first time in the history of computers that a mainstream processor has a full system on a single chip plus NPU for AI.

Is this the trend of the future? Will more and more chips get integrated and power efficient? Will digital transformation with AI also be driven by the marketing of big companies for speed. Will power efficiency and carbon footprint once again take a back seat? For the answers we will have to wait and see.

This time though the race is between America and China. Chinese companies like Huawei, Bitmain and Phytium have their own integrated chips based on ARM. The Chinese are betting on efficiency at a lower price while American companies use their marketing power to mesmerise us with the latest possibilities.
